Stanbery to Norton, February 28th, 1867; Copied by Walt Whitman

A copy of this letter was sent to Judge W.A. Carter

Attorney Generals Office

Washington

Feb. 28. 1867.

1Jesse O. Norton, Esq. U.S. Attorney.

Chicago. Ill.

Sir:

I enclose herewith a letter addressed to me by 2Mr. W.A. Carter of Utah Terr. in which I am informed that a murder was committed recently in Dakota Terr. of a merchant or peddler named 3Bicking, whose home was in Denver, Colorado Terr., (where his widow resides still,) by two men named 4Stickney and 3Black, (persons formerly belonging in Rochford and Chicago, respectively, in Illinois, where they are said to have opulent & influential relatives.)—the latter of whom has made full confession;—and that both are now in jail at Chicago, Ill., awaiting an examination, which is set for the 8th prose.,; And the proper requisition of the Executive authority of the Territory in which the murder was committed, & the proper warrant of extradition for trial in the courts of said Territory. The first Sec. of the Act of Congress of 12th Feb. 1793, (12 Stats [States] , 302,) seems to afford the Executive authority of the Territory in which the murder was committed, all the advice he could need for his action, upon one confirm:ation [confirmation] of the fact, But as so early a day has been fixed for the hearing, there is little probability that the proper requisition will be forthcoming in so short a time— & it is possible that A failure of justice might result from the absence of parties or counsel interested in favor of the prosecution.

Under these peculiar circumstances, you are hereby directed to act for the United States, & the Territory of Dakota, in the matter upon such Data as may be accessible to you, & procure such a commitment as shall subverse the ends of justice.

A copy of this letter is furnished to Mr. Carter, from whom you may expect further information & Testimony.

Respectfully, & Yours,

3Henry Stanbery

Attorney General A true copy, from original

Walt Whitman

Attorney General's Office.
1. Jesse O. Norton studied law in Illinois and was admitted to the bar in 1840. He served as a member of the Illinois House of Representatives in 1851 and 1852. He returned to the practice of law in 1866 and continued until 1869. He received correspondence from the Attorney General Henry Stanbery when he was practicing law. ↩

2.Judge Carter was to recieve a copy with the intention of testifying against George W. Black on behalf of the prosecution, while also recieving request for help from Black. ↩

3.Henry Stanbery was Ohio’s very first Attorney General from 1846 to 1851. He then became the United States Attorney General from 1866 to 1868. When this collection of correspondence was written, Stanbery was the United States Attorney General. Walt Whitman worked under Stanbery as a scribe. ↩
